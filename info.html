More Information

By Rio Miyakawa Baptista

Here is some information to expand your sonic experience and delve into the world of sound waves.

Sound is created by a vibrating source such as the vocal cords of humans and animals or the diaphragm in a speaker or the strings of a violin. These vibrations are then transferred to a medium where the particles in the medium vibrate, refracting and compressing producing a knock on effect (a bit like dominoes) and transferring the energy into the next particle while vibrating around a fixed point. The waves then create an expanding ripple and radiate in all directions away from the source to our ears, for example.
